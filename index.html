World Cup 2014 Dream Stream – 2.5 million views online with RTÉ Digital.

As well as being on TV, for this year's FIFA World Cup 2014, RTÉ streamed all 64 games live on www.rte.ie and RTÉ Player.

A quick survey* conducted post tournament has given us interesting insights into our football fans online viewing habits.

Download a PDF of the stats

*RTÉ Player survey of approx. 100 audience members (adults aged 15+, nationally rep.)

LIVE AND ON-DEMAND MATCH STREAMS

2.5m live and on-demand match streams in total, a 51% increase on the total from both the last World Cup and Euro 2012 combined – 700,000 for World Cup 2010 and 950,000 for Euro 2012.

Mobile trends

There was a huge increase in the use of mobile and tablet devices, with people using them as dual screens, and in some interesting places…

Nearly half (49%) watched a match on RTÉ Player/online at the same time as a different match on TV (Dual Screen)

Over a third of users (36%) watched on Player/online because their main TV was being used by someone else.

Most watched at home in the main living room (consistent with Player generally), but there were some interesting places people watched games – 40% watched in the bedroom, 12% in the kitchen, 8% in the garden, and 1 person ‘in the bath in the bathroom!’

keeping one eye on the ball

With the rise in mobile and tablet users there were some interesting insights into how people could fit in a live or on-demand match stream around their daily activities.

14% watched while cooking/ preparing meals

9% while doing household chores

4% while studying…

30% used social networks while watching
